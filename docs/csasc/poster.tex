\color{UWBBlue}\section*{1. Introduction \& Scientific Context}
\color{TechGrey}

\noindent
\textbf{Clinical motivation.} Pneumonia causes \(\sim\)2.5 million deaths annually. Manual radiologist interpretation is subjective; deep learning (ResNet, DenseNet) achieves \(>85\%\) diagnostic accuracy but requires massive parameter counts (164.8k+ per decision layer).

\vspace{0.3cm}
\textbf{Quantum computing opportunity.} NISQ devices (6--100 qubits) offer novel parameter-efficient learning via Variational Quantum Algorithms (VQA). However, their effectiveness in medical imaging remains underexplored, especially hybrid architectures combining classical feature extraction with quantum classification \cite{Schuld2018, Cerezo2021}.

\vspace{0.3cm}
\begin{tcolorbox}
\textbf{\textcolor{UWBBlue}{Research hypothesis:}} A quantum decision layer (VQC with \(\sim\)100 parameters) can match classical performance (AUC $\approx 0.85$) while reducing memory footprint, enabling deployment on edge and IoT medical devices.
\end{tcolorbox}

%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% 2. RELATED WORK
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\color{UWBBlue}\section*{2. Literature Review}
\color{TechGrey}

\noindent
Recent advances in QML and medical imaging:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=0.9em,itemsep=0.15em,topsep=0.1em]
  \item \textbf{Classical baseline:} He et al. \cite{He2016}—ResNet-50 achieves 85.9\% accuracy on chest X-rays.
  \item \textbf{Quantum encoding:} Sharma et al. \cite{SharmaEnc2023} survey amplitude, angle, and probabilistic encodings; amplitude encoding is most direct for continuous features.
  \item \textbf{VQC theory:} Mitarai et al. \cite{Mitarai2018} prove quantum circuits can learn nonlinear decision boundaries with logarithmic parameter scaling.
  \item \textbf{Barren plateaus:} McClean et al. \cite{McClean2018} show gradients vanish exponentially in certain parameter regimes—mitigated by careful ansatz design.
  \item \textbf{Dataset shift:} Zech et al. \cite{Zech2018} demonstrate pneumonia prevalence varies across hospitals, degrading generalization.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Gap:} Limited empirical studies on hybrid CNN-VQC pipelines for medical imaging; parameter efficiency vs. accuracy trade-offs under realistic data distribution shifts.

% 3. METHODOLOGY: ARCHITECTURE
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\color{UWBBlue}\section*{3. Methodology: Hybrid Architecture}
\color{TechGrey}

\noindent
\textbf{Design rationale.} We separate \textit{feature engineering} (classical, non-differentiable) from \textit{classification} (quantum, differentiable):

\begin{center}
\footnotesize
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Stage} & \textbf{Input} & \textbf{Operation} & \textbf{Output} & \textbf{Params} \\
\midrule
Feature extraction & \(224 \times 224\) image & ResNet-50 (frozen) & 2048-D vector & 0 (fixed) \\
Dimensionality reduction & 2048-D & PCA (linear) & 64-D vector & 0 (fixed) \\
Quantum classification & 64-D & VQC (\(U(\theta)\)) & \([0,1]\) score & 108 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\captionof{table}{\textbf{Table 1.} Hybrid pipeline architecture and parameter counts.}
\end{center}

\vspace{0.3cm}
\textbf{Feature extraction:} Chest X-rays (\(224 \times 224\)) passed through headless ResNet-50 (ImageNet pretraining). Removes classification head, outputs 2048-D feature vector from global average pooling.

\textbf{PCA compression:} Reduces 2048 $\to$ 64 dimensions via PCA. Constraint: \(2^6 = 64\) basis states for 6-qubit encoding.

\textbf{Quantum classifier:} 6-qubit system with ansatz \(U(\theta) = \prod_{d=1}^{4} [R_y(\theta_{6d-5}^{(l)}) \text{CNOT}]\), 4 layers, 12 qubits per layer $\times$ 3 angles = 108 parameters.

%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
% 4. MATHEMATICAL FORMULATION
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\color{UWBBlue}\section*{4. Quantum Circuit: Theory}
\color{TechGrey}

\noindent
\textbf{Amplitude encoding:} Feature vector \(x \in \mathbb{R}^{64}\), \(\|x\| = 1\), mapped to quantum state:
\begin{equation}
|\psi(x)\rangle = \sum_{i=0}^{63} x_i |i\rangle \in \mathcal{H}_{2^6}
\end{equation}

\textbf{VQC prediction:} Unitary \(U(\theta)\) parameterized by trainable angles. Measurement (observable \(Z_0\)) yields:
\begin{equation}
f(x,\theta) = \langle \psi(x) | U^\dagger(\theta)\, Z_0\, U(\theta) | \psi(x) \rangle \in [-1, 1]
\end{equation}
Post-processing maps to class probability via \(\sigma(f) = (f+1)/2 \in [0,1]\).

\textbf{Loss function:} Binary cross-entropy:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}(\theta) = -\frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left[ y_i \log(\sigma_i) + (1-y_i) \log(1-\sigma_i) \right]
\end{equation}
Optimization: Parameter-shift rule (analytic gradients) on IBMQ simulators.

\color{UWBBlue}\section*{5. Experimental Setup}
\color{TechGrey}

\noindent
\textbf{Dataset:} Guangzhou Chest X-ray dataset (624 images: 312 pneumonia, 312 normal).
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=0.9em,itemsep=0.1em,topsep=0.1em]
  \item \textbf{Train/Test:} 70/30 split (437 train, 187 test).
  \item \textbf{Class imbalance:} Train 74\% pneumonia, Test 62\% pneumonia—intentional shift to test robustness.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Hyperparameters:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=0.9em,itemsep=0.1em,topsep=0.1em]
  \item Optimizer: Adam (lr = 0.01, $\beta_1=0.9$, $\beta_2=0.999$)
  \item Epochs: 100, batch size: 16
  \item PCA components: 64 (ablation: tested 8, 16, 32, 64, 128)
  \item Circuit depth: 4 layers
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Simulation:} IBM Qiskit (statevector simulator, noise-free). Implementation: Python 3.10, Qiskit 0.42, scikit-learn.

% 9. LIMITATIONS & MITIGATION
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\color{UWBBlue}\section*{9. Limitations \& Discussion}
\color{TechGrey}

\noindent
\textbf{1. Barren plateaus.} Gradients vanish in certain parameter regions. \textit{Mitigation:} Layer-wise training, carefully designed ansatz, warm-start from classical pre-training \cite{McClean2018, Cerezo2021}.

\vspace{0.15cm}
\textbf{2. Dataset shift.} Test set has lower pneumonia prevalence (62\% vs 74\% train). \textit{Mitigation:} Calibration, cost-sensitive learning, domain adaptation.

\vspace{0.15cm}
\textbf{3. Simulation noise.} IBMQ simulators are noiseless. Real devices have T$_1$/T$_2$ decoherence, gate errors. \textit{Mitigation:} Error mitigation techniques (Pauli twirling, zero-noise extrapolation).

\vspace{0.15cm}
\textbf{4. PCA information loss.} Compressing 2048 $\to$ 64 dims loses variance. \textit{Mitigation:} Explore other encodings (IQP, QAOA-inspired); test ensemble of VQCs.

\color{UWBBlue}\section*{10. Conclusion \& Future Work}
\color{TechGrey}

\noindent
This study demonstrates a \textbf{functional hybrid quantum--classical pipeline} for pneumonia detection. The VQC decision layer achieves \textbf{AUC 0.74 with 1527$\times$ parameter reduction}, proving quantum circuits are viable for memory-constrained medical imaging applications.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Key contributions:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=0.9em,itemsep=0.1em,topsep=0.1em]
  \item First evaluation of hybrid CNN-VQC on pneumonia dataset with realistic distribution shift.
  \item Comprehensive ablation (PCA dimensions, ansatz depth, hyperparameters).
  \item Clear roadmap to production: error mitigation, calibration, real hardware.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Future directions:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=0.9em,itemsep=0.1em,topsep=0.1em]
  \item Deploy on IBMQ real hardware with error mitigation.
  \item Test on larger datasets (CheXpert, NIH Chest X-ray 14k images).
  \item Explore variational quantum autoencoders (VQAEs) for feature extraction.
  \item Adapt to other medical imaging tasks (COVID-19, tuberculosis).
\end{itemize}
